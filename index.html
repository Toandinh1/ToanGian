About Me

I hold a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering from Hanoi University of Science and Technology (HUST). My academic foundation was solidified through hands-on experience at the LSP Lab at HUST, where I researched advanced models and technologies for 5G communication systems. In 2021, I joined the AIoT Lab at Phenikaa University as a research assistant under advisor Dr. Thien Van Luong, focusing on applying ML-based signal detection in wireless communication systems. In 2022, I undertook a six-month internship at Bosch Vietnam. Since July 2023, I have been contributing as a research assistant at the ICC Lab of VinUniversity under advisor Asst. Prof. Van Dinh Nguyen, where my primary research focuses on leveraging AI/ML and wireless sensing technologies for human pose estimation. Currently, I am pursuing a PhD program at Northeastern University, US, under supervisor Asst. Prof. Francesco Restuccia with a research focus on applying AI/ML in next-generation multi-agent control systems

Research Overview

My research currently focuses on designing efficient multi-agent systems with integrated communication, especially under constrained-resource scenarios. In addition, I am interested in WiFi-based human pose estimation. I welcome collaborations in these areas.
